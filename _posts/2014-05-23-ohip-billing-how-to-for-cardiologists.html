Date written: 13/03/2014

This blog is intended to teach the basics of OHIP billing for cardiologists working in a hospital setting. Generally, as a cardiologist, a patient will be referred to you for a consult. To bill for this first initial consult, you need to determine the following for your initial consult:</span>

 </span>

Location</strong> (where did your initial consult take place?): ER; Ward</span>

Type</strong> (the type of consult): General; Comprehensive; Limited; Repeat
Time</strong> (time of day you did the consult): Weekday 7:00-17:00; Weekday 7:00-17:00 during Office Hours; Evening Weekday 17:00-24:00; Weekend/Holidays 7:00-24:00; Nights 00:00-7:00
Travel </strong>(did you have to travel to go see the patient): Yes; No</span>

Depending on your answers from above we can determine which fee codes to bill to the Ministry of Health for the initial consult. Using your answers follow the breakdown below to find out which codes you need to bill. The first half is for ER visits while the second half covers Ward visits.

That covers your fee codes for the initial visit with the patient. Now what happens if you continue to visit the patient daily until they are discharged?</span>

You can use the following fee codes scheme:</span>

</div>
 

Initial visit:</strong> determined using the logic above</span>

</div>
 

Day after admission:</strong> C122A + E083A (The E083A is the premium if you are the most responsible physician for the patient)</span>

</div>
 

Day 2 after admission:</strong> C123A +E083A</span>

</div>
 

From Day 3 after admission to discharge:</strong> (Note that you use a different code for these subsequent visits, depending on when you are seeing the patient relative to their admission date. There are restrictions on how many times you can use C607A and C609A).</span>

</div>
 

-          </span></span>C602A (if 5 weeks from admission) +E083A</span></span>

</div>
 

-          </span></span>C607A (if 6-13 weeks from admission) + E083A</span></span>

</div>
 

-          </span></span>C609A (if past 13 weeks from admission) + E083A</span></span>

</div>
 

Discharge:</strong> C124A + E083A</span>

</div>
